Open Work

Our public work includes the dataset, the benchmark standard, and the tooling we use ourselves. Each of these exists because the inference ecosystem asked for it and nobody had shipped it yet.

Touchdown-1

An open dataset of realistic agentic coding, multi-turn chat, reasoning, and tool-use traces — the workload public inference benchmarks have been explicitly missing. Reproducible scripts for vLLM, SGLang, and LMCache on H100, H200, and B200.

ISB-1

An open benchmark standard for inference measurement. Reproducible, workload-aware, and designed to be cited — built to give operators, neoclouds, and framework maintainers a common language for performance.

InferScope

Open tooling for inspecting and diagnosing live inference systems. Used in our own engagements and published for the broader community of operators running production AI infrastructure.

Direction

We start with the open dataset, the benchmark, and the tools operators need today. Over time, each lane deepens into more serious infrastructure — first software, then applied services, then hardware.

01

Today — Open benchmarks, kernels, and operator training

Touchdown-1 dataset, ISB-1 benchmark standard, FP4 KV-cache bring-up work, AMD MI355X MFMA kernels, and the first operator course. Paid private benchmark engagements for neoclouds and model labs.

02

Next — Production-grade inference systems and managed deployments

Deeper applied systems work, managed inference deployments for customers, continuous benchmarking infrastructure, and a larger cohort of operator training anchored on the open dataset and benchmark.

03

Long Term — KV-cache accelerator hardware and US silicon

Designing and manufacturing specialized inference silicon in the United States — starting with KV-cache accelerators informed by years of workload data from our own benchmarks and engagements — and helping build the data centers that support them.

About Touchdown Labs

Touchdown Labs builds the infrastructure, education, and applied systems behind the AI-native economy. The same team that ships the open dataset and benchmark also writes the course, runs the private engagements, and does the kernel work. The loop is the product.

Our work sits at the intersection of three things the industry needs and rarely gets together: realistic public benchmarks, honest operator training, and applied engineering that actually runs in production — not demos.

We are currently part of GMI Cloud's SCALE program and running our own operations as a design partner for General Intelligence Company's Cofounder platform. We publish openly, charge for serious work, and teach what we learn along the way.

Long term, we want to go beyond software into designing and manufacturing specialized inference silicon in the United States and helping build the data centers that support it. The software work is how we earn the right to build the hardware.
